Setup

Define the experiment environment — what data is fed into the simulation. These bound the analysis but aren't varied by the optimiser.

Market parameters optimised

The L2 strategy levers — DA withholding, ID-forecast trust, mFRR↔aFRR per-direction split. The "Optimise market" button sweeps over these while holding the oversell strategy fixed at its current values.

Intra-day oversell optimised

S3: for each ISP, if da_sold < "Skip if DA sold ≥" AND (VWAP1H − rolling-mean p_mfrr) ≥ S_min AND recent σ ≤ σ_max, sell up to X_cap MW on intraday and submit a hedge mFRR-dn bid at VWAP1H + M — a stop-loss mFRR-dn offer the park wouldn't normally place (pitched above the typical clearing). The hedge clears whenever p_mfrr ≤ VWAP1H + M, bounding the loss on the oversold MW. Rolling stats are over [target − K − L, target − L) — the publication lag L (default 4 ISPs ≈ 1 h) models the fact that an intra-day trader cannot see settlement prices of the ISPs immediately preceding the target. Set X_cap = 0 to disable S3 and revert L3 to L2 behaviour. The "Optimise oversell" button sweeps 4 S3 params (K, S_min, σ_max, M) while holding market params, L, and X_cap fixed — X_cap stays at the user-set value because the backtest has no price-impact term and a sweep would always pick the grid boundary.
