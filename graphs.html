Define the experiment environment: which ISPs are fed into the graphs, how to tame extreme prices, and how many buckets each axis is split into.

Bucket counts x-axis

Each x-axis on the graphs below is a quantile binning over the selected window. Increase the count for finer granularity.

Spread by Baltic Wind Forecast — by regime

Box plots of the mFRR spread (PmFRR − PDA), split into wind-forecast quantile bins. Surplus (left) shows the spread when the system was over-generating; deficit (right) when it was short. Bin counts and boundaries are computed from the current simulation window.

Spread by Baltic Solar Forecast — by regime

Same idea as the wind-binned plots, but using the Baltic-level solar day-ahead forecast as the x-axis.

Spread by Wind × Solar — by regime

Median spread for every (wind bin × solar bin) cell. Each cell shows the median, mean, σ and sample count. Cells with no observations are blank.

|Spread| by Wind Level — Matched by DA Price Band

One panel per DA-price quantile band. Within each panel, the Baltic wind forecast is split into Low / Mid / High by global quantiles (so panel-internal counts can be uneven). The y-axis is the absolute value of the spread, so this view shows volatility rather than direction.

|Spread| by Solar Level — Matched by DA Price Band

Same layout as the wind version, but the level axis splits ISPs by Baltic solar day-ahead forecast (sum of EE + LV + LT). Useful for spotting whether high-irradiance hours co-move with mFRR volatility differently from wind.

|Spread| by Renewables Level — Matched by DA Price Band

Combines wind + solar into a single Baltic renewables axis (sum of EE + LV + LT day-ahead wind and solar forecasts). Shows how mFRR volatility depends on total VRE penetration once DA price is held constant.

All math runs client-side. Parameters are quantile-driven: changing bucket counts re-derives the bin boundaries from the data.
