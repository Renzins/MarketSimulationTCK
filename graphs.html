aFRR activation rate — by Latvia imbalance

For every 4-second aFRR slot in the chosen window, count whether any direction had a price (activated) or both prices were missing (not activated). Bars are split by the LV-only imbalance regime — so we're asking "does aFRR fire more during Latvian surplus or Latvian deficit?"

aFRR activation rate — by Baltic imbalance

Same data as the previous chart, but classified by the full Baltic imbalance volume (LV + EE + LT). Comparing this chart with the Latvia-only one tells you whether aFRR activation tracks the Latvian view or the wider Baltic view.

aFRR activation rate — when LV and the rest of Baltic disagree

Looks specifically at periods where Latvia's imbalance and the rest of the Baltic (EE + LT) point in opposite directions — i.e. the synchronous area is split. Two bars: LV+ / rest− (Latvia surplus while EE+LT in deficit) and LV− / rest+ (Latvia deficit while EE+LT in surplus). If aFRR direction tracks the dominant area, the bars favour the colour matching the rest-of-Baltic sign; if it tracks Latvia specifically, they favour the LV sign. Uses the LV thresholds plus a separate rest-of-Baltic pair (default ±20 MW — EE+LT alone is ≈ 2/3 the magnitude of full Baltic).

Setup

Summary statistics

Headline numbers: how often each market fires, how often they fire in agreement, and the Pearson correlation between the two spreads (where both are defined — i.e. mFRR cleared AND aFRR-up had favourable activity in the same ISP).

Joint direction matrix

Rows: mFRR direction inferred from p_mfrr sign (Up ≥ +1, Dead band |·| < 1, Down ≤ −1). Columns: aFRR direction inferred from avg_p_pos / avg_p_neg being non-zero (Up only / Down only / Both directions / Neither). Diagonal-like cells (Up–Up, Down–Down) mean the markets agree.

Sign agreement of the DA-spread

For each 4-s aFRR slot where the directional clearing is defined, we record the sign of mFRR spread (= p_mfrr − p_da, broadcast from the encompassing ISP) and the sign of aFRR spread (= AST_POS − p_da on the left chart, AST_NEG − p_da on the right). Four mutually-exclusive buckets: both positive (both markets above DA → upward signal), both negative (below DA → downward signal), or mixed. Note on data: these slot-level charts use the raw per-4-s AST_POS / AST_NEG clearings — the favourable-only filter (which drops unfavourable slots) is applied only in the L3 P&L backtest, not here. So "aFRR-down" can land in any of the four buckets, including both positive (this happens when AST_NEG itself cleared above DA — the system priced curtailment at a level the wind park wouldn't bid into for profit, but the market still cleared there). During the brief window where the page falls back to per-ISP averages (while the chunked 4-s file is still loading), the favourable-filtered values DO force the right chart into "both negative" — that fallback distribution is not market truth.

mFRR / DA spread when aFRR is N/A

For 4-s slots where the corresponding aFRR direction was not activated (the AST_{POS,NEG} field was null upstream), the sign-agreement chart above can't compare two spreads — there's only the mFRR price. These charts show how mFRR positioned itself vs DA in those silent-aFRR slots: above DA (green), near DA within ±10% (yellow), or below DA (red). Together with the gray N/A slice in the chart above, they answer: "when aFRR didn't fire, was mFRR signalling anything?"

Spread correlation scatter

Each point is one ISP plotted at (mFRR spread, aFRR spread). A clean diagonal cloud means the two markets move together; a flat or anti-diagonal cloud means they're uncorrelated or inverse. Left: aFRR-up direction; right: aFRR-down direction. Subsampled to ≤ 8 k points for responsiveness.
