Spread by Baltic Wind Forecast — by regime

Box plots of the mFRR spread (PmFRR − PDA), split into wind-forecast quantile bins. Surplus (left) shows the spread when the system was over-generating; deficit (right) when it was short. Bin counts and boundaries are computed from the current simulation window.

Spread by Baltic Solar Forecast — by regime

Same idea as the wind-binned plots, but using the Baltic-level solar day-ahead forecast as the x-axis.

Spread by Wind × Solar — by regime

Median spread for every (wind bin × solar bin) cell. Each cell shows the median, mean, σ and sample count. Cells with no observations are blank.

|Spread| by Wind Level — Matched by DA Price Band

One panel per DA-price quantile band. Within each panel, the Baltic wind forecast is split into Low / Mid / High by global quantiles (so panel-internal counts can be uneven). The y-axis is the absolute value of the spread, so this view shows volatility rather than direction.

|Spread| by Solar Level — Matched by DA Price Band

Same layout as the wind version, but the level axis splits ISPs by Baltic solar day-ahead forecast (sum of EE + LV + LT). Useful for spotting whether high-irradiance hours co-move with mFRR volatility differently from wind.

|Spread| by Renewables Level — Matched by DA Price Band

Combines wind + solar into a single Baltic renewables axis (sum of EE + LV + LT day-ahead wind and solar forecasts). Shows how mFRR volatility depends on total VRE penetration once DA price is held constant.

Setup

Same shape of parameters as the mFRR section (date range, winsorization, regime thresholds) — but the date range is clamped to the aFRR data extent (2025-05-01 onward) and a separate set of LV-imbalance thresholds is added so the "by Latvia" vs "by Baltic" charts can use different cuts.

aFRR activation rate — by Latvia imbalance

For every 4-second aFRR slot in the chosen window, count whether any direction had a price (activated) or both prices were missing (not activated). Bars are split by the LV-only imbalance regime — so we're asking "does aFRR fire more during Latvian surplus or Latvian deficit?"

aFRR activation rate — by Baltic imbalance

Same data as the previous chart, but classified by the full Baltic imbalance volume (LV + EE + LT). Comparing this chart with the Latvia-only one tells you whether aFRR activation tracks the Latvian view or the wider Baltic view.

aFRR activation rate — when LV and the rest of Baltic disagree

Looks specifically at periods where Latvia's imbalance and the rest of the Baltic (EE + LT) point in opposite directions — i.e. the synchronous area is split. Two bars: LV+ / rest− (Latvia surplus while EE+LT in deficit) and LV− / rest+ (Latvia deficit while EE+LT in surplus). If aFRR direction tracks the dominant area, the bars favour the colour matching the rest-of-Baltic sign; if it tracks Latvia specifically, they favour the LV sign. Uses the LV thresholds plus a separate rest-of-Baltic pair (default ±20 MW — EE+LT alone is ≈ 2/3 the magnitude of full Baltic).
